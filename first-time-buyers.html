First-Time Homebuyer Programs in Ontario

A complete guide to the grants, rebates, and incentives that can put $100,000+ toward your first home—and how to actually qualify for them.

Most First-Time Buyers Leave Money on the Table

Here's what usually happens: you walk into a bank, they run some numbers, and they offer you a mortgage. You assume that's the best you can do. You sign. Done.

But here's what you don't see: there are grants, rebates, incentives, and programs specifically designed to help first-time buyers—and most people never hear about them. Between federal programs, Ontario-specific rebates, and local assistance, you could be looking at $100,000 or more in combined support.

That's not an exaggeration. That's what happens when you know where to look—and when you work with someone whose job is to find every advantage for your specific situation.

For a $500,000 new build in Simcoe County, a first-time buyer could potentially access: $40,000 FHSA + $60,000 RRSP HBP + $50,000 Simcoe County loan + $4,000 land transfer rebate + $25,000+ GST rebate + $1,500 tax credit = over $180,000 in combined support.

Federal Programs: The Foundation of Your Down Payment

First Home Savings Account (FHSA)

Up to $40,000

Tax-Free Down Payment Savings

The FHSA is the best savings vehicle for first-time buyers—period. You get the tax deduction of an RRSP (contributions reduce your taxable income) and the tax-free withdrawals of a TFSA (no tax when you use it for your home). No other account offers both.

  • Annual contribution limit: $8,000
  • Lifetime maximum: $40,000
  • No repayment required (unlike the RRSP Home Buyers' Plan)
  • Must use within 15 years or transfer to RRSP
  • Available to: Canadian residents 18+ who haven't owned a home in the past 4 years

RRSP Home Buyers' Plan (HBP)

Up to $60,000

Tax-Free RRSP Withdrawal ($120,000 for Couples)

Withdraw from your RRSP tax-free to buy your first home. The limit increased from $35,000 to $60,000 in April 2024—a significant boost for buyers in expensive markets.

  • Individual limit: $60,000
  • Couples buying together: $120,000 combined
  • Repayment: Must repay over 15 years (starts 2-5 years after withdrawal)
  • Requirement: Funds must be in RRSP for at least 90 days before withdrawal

First-Time Home Buyer Tax Credit

$1,500

Non-Refundable Tax Credit

A straightforward $1,500 back on your taxes in the year you purchase. Claim it on your tax return—no application required.

30-Year Amortization for First-Time Buyers

As of December 2024, first-time buyers (and anyone buying new construction) can now access 30-year amortizations on insured mortgages. Previously, only 25-year terms were available for high-ratio mortgages. This extends your payments over a longer period, reducing monthly costs by approximately $300-600 per month on a typical mortgage—though you'll pay more interest over the life of the loan.

Increased Insured Mortgage Cap

Also effective December 2024, the insured mortgage limit increased from $1 million to $1.5 million. This means buyers in expensive markets can now access insured mortgages (with as little as 5% down) on properties up to $1.5M—previously, anything over $1M required 20% down.

Ontario-Specific Programs

Ontario Land Transfer Tax Rebate

Up to $4,000

Provincial LTT Rebate for First-Time Buyers

First-time buyers in Ontario receive a rebate of up to $4,000 on the provincial land transfer tax. This covers the full LTT on homes up to $368,000, with partial rebates on higher-priced homes.

Toronto Land Transfer Tax Rebate (Toronto Only)

Up to $4,475

Municipal LTT Rebate for First-Time Buyers in Toronto

Toronto has its own municipal land transfer tax on top of the provincial one. First-time buyers get an additional rebate of up to $4,475, for a combined maximum of $8,475 in LTT rebates when buying in Toronto.

New GST/HST Rebates on New Builds

Recent and proposed changes have dramatically expanded GST/HST relief for new construction purchases:

Rebate Maximum Notes
Federal GST Rebate (proposed) Up to $50,000 100% GST rebate on new homes up to $1M
Ontario HST Rebate (proposed) Up to $80,000 100% provincial HST on new homes up to $1M
Existing Ontario HST Rebate Up to $24,000 75% of provincial HST, available now

Note: Some proposed rebates are pending legislation. I stay current on what's actually available versus what's been announced.

Simcoe County Down Payment Assistance

Up to $50,000

10% Forgivable Loan for Local Residents

This is the program most people don't know about. Simcoe County offers a 20-year forgivable loan covering up to 10% of your purchase price—if you stay in the home for 20 years, you never repay a cent.

  • Maximum: $50,000 (10% of purchase price)
  • Purchase price limit: $712,300
  • Household income limit: $121,500
  • Requirements: Must be current Simcoe County resident (including Barrie & Orillia), currently renting
  • Waitlist: 6-12 months typical—apply early
  • Contact: 705-725-7215 x1119

Important: The Simcoe County program has income and price limits that exclude some buyers. But if you qualify, it's essentially free money toward your down payment. I can help you determine eligibility and coordinate with other programs.

Mortgage Qualification: What Lenders Actually Look At

The Stress Test

Every mortgage applicant must qualify at the higher of their actual contract rate plus 2%, or 5.25%—whichever is greater. This "stress test" ensures you can afford payments if rates rise. With current rates around 4-5%, most buyers qualify at 6-7%.

This means you need more income to qualify than you might expect based on actual payments. A broker can calculate your exact qualification amount before you start shopping.

Debt Service Ratios

Ratio Maximum What It Measures
GDS (Gross Debt Service) 39% Housing costs ÷ gross income
TDS (Total Debt Service) 44% All debt payments ÷ gross income

The TDS calculation uses the 3% rule for credit cards and lines of credit—lenders assume 3% of the balance as your monthly payment, regardless of your actual minimum. A $15,000 credit card balance counts as $450/month in your qualification, even if your minimum payment is much lower.

500-650 B-lenders 20%
Below 500 Private lenders 25-35%

Down Payment Requirements

The minimum down payment in Canada depends on purchase price:

Purchase Price Minimum Down Payment
Up to $500,000 5%
$500,001 - $1,500,000 5% on first $500K + 10% on remainder
Over $1,500,000 20%

Example: For a $700,000 home, minimum down payment = (5% × $500,000) + (10% × $200,000) = $25,000 + $20,000 = $45,000

Acceptable Down Payment Sources

  • Personal savings
  • FHSA withdrawals
  • RRSP Home Buyers' Plan withdrawals
  • Gifted funds from immediate family (gift letter required)
  • Proceeds from selling existing property
  • Simcoe County down payment assistance program

Common Myths That Hold First-Time Buyers Back

"I need 20% down to buy a home."
False. You can buy with as little as 5% down on homes up to $500,000, and the new $1.5M insured cap means even expensive properties are accessible with less than 20%.

"My credit isn't good enough."
The average Canadian credit score is 762. You need 600 for insured mortgages, and B-lenders work with scores in the 500s. Poor credit doesn't mean no mortgage—it means different options.

"Student debt disqualifies me."
Student loans affect your TDS ratio, but they don't disqualify you. Strategic debt paydown before applying can significantly increase your purchasing power.

"Renting is cheaper than owning."
With current rent prices in Simcoe County, monthly mortgage payments on a starter home are often comparable to—or lower than—rent, while building equity.

"I should go to my bank."
Your bank shows you their products. A broker shows you 50+ lenders' products—including options with better rates, better terms, and qualification flexibility your bank doesn't offer.

Your Next Steps

  1. Open an FHSA immediately if you haven't already—you can contribute $8,000 this year and carry forward unused room.
  2. Check your credit score through Borrowell (free Equifax) or Credit Karma (free TransUnion).
  3. Calculate your debt service ratios to understand your approximate budget.
  4. Talk to a broker—not to commit, but to understand your actual options and timeline.

Every situation is different. Some buyers are ready now but don't realize it. Others think they're ready but need 6-12 months of preparation. A conversation costs nothing and gives you clarity either way.

Credit Repair & Rebuilding in Ontario

Poor credit doesn't mean no mortgage. It means different options—and a roadmap to get where you want to be.

Credit Challenges Don't Disqualify You

Rebuilding damaged credit to mortgage-qualifying territory typically takes 12-24 months of consistent positive behavior. But here's what most people don't realize: you may not need to wait that long.

B-lenders work with credit scores in the 500s. Private lenders focus on equity rather than credit entirely. And for those who want to reach prime lending (A-lenders with best rates), there's a clear path with predictable timelines.

Understanding Credit Score Ranges

Canadian credit scores range from 300 to 900. Here's what each range means for mortgage options:

The average Canadian credit score is 762. If you're below that, you're not alone—and there are clear steps to improve.

Payment history and utilization together account for 65% of your score. Focus here for the fastest improvements.

How Long Negative Items Stay on Your Report

  • Late payments: 6 years from date reported
  • Collections: 6 years from date of first default
  • Consumer proposal: 3 years after completion OR 6 years from filing (whichever comes first)
  • Bankruptcy (first time): 6-7 years after discharge (varies by bureau and province)
  • Bankruptcy (second+): 14 years
  • Hard inquiries: 3-6 years (impact on score fades after ~12 months)

Credit Rebuilding Strategies That Actually Work in Canada

1. Dispute Errors First (Fastest Win)

Studies suggest 30-34% of Canadian credit reports contain at least one error. Disputing inaccuracies is the fastest legal way to boost your score. Both Equifax and TransUnion offer free online dispute processes—investigations must be completed within 30 days.

You can dispute: accounts you don't recognize, incorrect payment histories, wrong balances, and items that should have aged off after 6 years.

2. Utilization Optimization (30-60 Day Impact)

Credit card companies report balances once monthly (usually on statement date). Paying down balances before that date reports lower utilization to bureaus.

Example: Someone with $8,000 in credit limits carrying $6,000 (75% utilization) could see meaningful improvement within 30-60 days by paying down to $2,000 (25% utilization).

Target: Keep utilization below 30%. Ideal: 10-20%.

3. Secured Credit Cards

When regular cards aren't accessible, secured cards provide a path. Your deposit serves as collateral—make purchases, pay on time, build history.

  • Neo Secured Mastercard: $50 minimum deposit
  • Refresh Secured Card: $200 deposit, reports to both bureaus, no annual fee

4. Credit Builder Loans

  • Borrowell Credit Builder: $5-25 bi-weekly, no credit check
  • KOHO Credit Building: $5-10 monthly
  • Spring Financial Foundation: Results in $750 saved at term end

Canada-specific note: Becoming an authorized user on someone else's credit card does NOT help build credit in Canada. Unlike the US, Canadian credit bureaus don't report authorized user accounts to your credit file. This popular American strategy doesn't work here.

Mortgage Options at Each Credit Level

A-Lenders (680+ Credit)

Major banks, credit unions, monoline lenders. Best rates, lowest costs, full stress test applies. Minimum 5% down for insured mortgages.

B-Lenders (500-650 Credit)

Home Trust, Equitable Bank, MERIX/NPX, Haventree Bank—accessible through brokers. Rates typically 1-2% higher than prime, plus ~1% lender fee. Minimum 20% down required (mortgage insurance not available).

Private Lenders (Below 500 or Recent Bankruptcy)

Focus on equity rather than credit. Rates between 6-15%, fees of 3-6%, terms of 1-3 years. Require substantial equity (25-35% down). Used as bridge financing while rebuilding for refinance into better terms.

The broker advantage: A mortgage broker can map your current credit situation to available lenders, create a realistic timeline to better options, and structure your application to maximize approval chances at each stage.

What Credit Repair Companies Can (and Can't) Do

Credit repair companies operating in Canada face strict legal limitations:

  • They cannot remove accurate negative information
  • They cannot charge upfront fees (prohibited by Canadian consumer protection law)
  • They receive no special treatment from credit bureaus

Everything a credit repair company can legally do, you can do yourself. File disputes directly with Equifax (1-866-828-5961) and TransUnion (1-800-663-9980).

Better alternative: Non-profit credit counselling agencies like Credit Canada or Credit Counselling Society offer free guidance, budget analysis, and debt management programs.

Free Credit Monitoring Resources

  • Borrowell: Free weekly Equifax credit scores and full reports (borrowell.com)
  • Credit Karma Canada: Free TransUnion scores and monitoring (creditkarma.ca)
  • Equifax: Free monthly access through myEquifax accounts
  • TransUnion: Free Consumer Disclosure through their website

Use both Borrowell and Credit Karma to see both bureau files—not all creditors report to both, so your scores may differ.

Your Next Steps

  1. Check your credit scores through free services (Borrowell for Equifax, Credit Karma for TransUnion)
  2. Review your reports for errors and dispute anything inaccurate
  3. Identify quick wins—high utilization is fastest to fix
  4. Talk to a broker about your current options—you may qualify for more than you think

Credit challenges don't have to mean indefinite delay of homeownership. With proper guidance and the right lending partners, the path from damaged credit to mortgage approval is measurable and achievable.
